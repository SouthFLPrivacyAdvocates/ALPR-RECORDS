FINAL ASSESSMENT
================================================================================

The City of Plantation has implemented ALPR/LPR systems as a city-wide public 
safety initiative. These systems are controlled and funded at the City Council 
level, not at the district level.

The Midtown Advisory Board APPEARS TO HAVE NO DIRECT ROLE in ALPR deployment 
decisions, funding, or oversight. However:

- Midtown District may benefit from shared city ALPR capabilities
- Some ALPR equipment (park cameras) may be located within Midtown boundaries
- Midtown District budgets may contribute indirectly through city-wide revenues

Evidence that Midtown is NOT directly involved in ALPR:
- No Midtown-specific ALPR procurement found
- Midtown board minutes contain no surveillance/camera discussion
- Midtown budgets show no ALPR line items
- ALPR procurement driven by Police Department/City Council, not districts

Evidence that Gateway District IS engaged with LPR:
- Gateway board minutes explicitly discuss "license plate camera funding"
- Gateway mentions LPR on traffic lights
- Gateway District appears to have more active surveillance initiatives
