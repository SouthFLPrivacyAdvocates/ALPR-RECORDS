"Supply two (2) LPR Speed Trailers and the accompanying camera data access/software
for the City of Plantation Police Department" in an amount not to exceed $79,410.00.
Item Description:
This system provides access to Vigilant Solutions shared data base. It will give the
Police Department access to over 24 million detections per month and serve as an
investigative data platform to benefit our investigators, analyst and detectives. In
addition to the data base this purchase order will provide two mobile license plate
recognition (LPR) speed trailers. These trailers will provide nearby police officers, as
well as dispatch with alerts for stolen vehicles, felony vehicles or other criminal elements
entering or within the City of Plantation.
The Procurement Section secured informal competitive bids in accordance with section
2-226(b) of the City's code for the above mentioned equipment. This informal bid (WB
No. 019-17) was advertised for 27 days on the City's and Demand Star websites. This
solicitation had 21 planholders and 370 suppliers were notified.

RESOLUTION NO. _________
A RESOLUTION OF THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF PLANTATION,
FLORIDA, TO SEEK FUNDING FROM THE U. S. DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE
FY 2017 EDWARD BYRNE MEMORIAL JUSTICE ASSISTANCE GRANT (JAG)
THROUGH THE BROWARD SHERIFF’S OFFICE.
WHEREAS, the City of Plantation Police Department desires to seek funding from
the 2017 Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant (JAG) in the amount of
$21,743.00; and
WHEREAS, the City of Plantation Police Department agrees to allow the Broward
Sheriff’s Office (BSO) to serve as the lead agent and to make application on the
departments behalf and to use 8.62% of the award for grant administration, which
is BSO’s U.S. Department of Justice approved indirect cost allowance; and
WHEREAS, the City of Plantation Police Department will use the grant funds for
the purchase and installation of License Plate Recognition (LPR) cameras at two
City parks.
WHEREAS, the City of Plantation Police Department has until September 30,
2018, to complete their grants projects and there is no matching fund requirement.
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY
OF PLANTATION, FLORIDA, that:
SECTION 1: The City Council hereby authorizes the Mayor to seek funding for this
grant and assures compliance with all legal and administrative requirements that
govern the City for acceptance and use of federal grant funds.
SECTION 2: The City Council hereby authorizes the Mayor, upon receipt, to sign
the Memorandum of Understanding between Scott Israel, Sheriff of Broward
County and the City of Plantation.
SECTION 3: This Resolution shall take effect immediately upon passage by the
City Council and signature by the Mayor.

Mission Statement
It is the mission of the Plantation Fire Department to protect the lives and property of the citizens of
Plantation with the highest levels of knowledge and skill in the spirit of “neighbor helping neighbor”
upon which the Fire Department was founded, while recognizing our fiscal responsibility to the
community.
Department Description
The Plantation Fire Department has been serving the citizens of Plantation since its inception in 1957
as an all-volunteer fire department. In 1996 the Department added an Emergency Medical Services
Division in order to provide the citizens with improved emergency medical care. Today the Plantation
Fire Department is the largest combination fire department in Florida with an insurance rating of two
(2). It is a multi-faceted community service agency providing a variety of services including fire
suppression, emergency medical services, fire and life safety, fleet & facility maintenance, public
outreach and emergency management.

Mission Statement
Our Mission is to improve the quality of life for the citizens of Plantation through a partnership with the
community that promotes safe, secure streets and neighborhoods. We recognize our responsibility to

Department Description
The Plantation Police Department is a full-service law enforcement agency dedicated to providing the
highest level of service possible to our community. Our department is one of the few selected agencies
to have earned accreditation for both the International Accrediting Agency (CALEA) and the State of
Florida Accrediting Agency (CFA).
The major responsibilities and services provided include:
Records Section: some of the responsibilities of the unit consist of reviewing and approving
police reports, filing cases with the State Attorney’s Office, processing public record requests,
performing background checks, fingerprinting, accepting and distributing subpoenas and many
other responsibilities.
Communications Unit: process and handle service call for Police, Fire and EMS Services.
Road Patrol: provide law enforcement services based on areas of responsibility 24 hours a day
and 7 days a week.
The Investigations Division conducts follow-up investigations involving criminal cases and other
incidents in the City of Plantation. The CID may also take up complicated cases like fraud and
identity theft, narcotics violations, property crimes or very complicated persons’ crimes cases
like homicide.
The Canine Unit (K-9) supplements the Department’s Road Patrol Division by providing support
to in-progress incidents. K-9 Officers and dogs are specially trained to locate subjects and
property. The K-9 Unit also conducts demonstrations at a variety of community events.
Field Force: restores order, move crowds, rescues victims and isolates problem areas.
Special Weapons and Tactics Team (SWAT): responsible for responding to critical incidents and
executing warrants.

Mission Statement
Our Mission is to improve the quality of life for the citizens of Plantation through a partnership with the
community that promotes safe, secure streets and neighborhoods. We recognize our responsibility to
maintain order, while affording dignity and respect to every individual.
Department Description
The Plantation Police Department is a full-service law enforcement agency dedicated to providing the
highest level of service possible to our community. Our department is one of the few selected agencies
to have earned accreditation for both the International Accrediting Agency (CALEA) and the State of
Florida Accrediting Agency (CFA).
The major responsibilities and services provided include:
Records Section: some of the responsibilities of the unit consist of reviewing and approving
police reports, filing cases with the State Attorney’s Office, processing public record requests,
performing background checks, fingerprinting, accepting and distributing subpoenas and many
other responsibilities.
Communications Unit: process and handle service call for Police, Fire and EMS Services.
Road Patrol: provide law enforcement services based on areas of responsibility 24 hours a day
and 7 days a week.
The Investigations Division conducts follow-up investigations involving criminal cases and other
incidents in the City of Plantation. The CID may also take up complicated cases like fraud and
identity theft, narcotics violations, property crimes or very complicated persons’ crimes cases
like homicide.
The Canine Unit (K-9) supplements the Department’s Road Patrol Division by providing support
to in-progress incidents. K-9 Officers and dogs are specially trained to locate subjects and
property. The K-9 Unit also conducts demonstrations at a variety of community events.
Field Force: restores order, move crowds, rescues victims and isolates problem areas.
Special Weapons and Tactics Team (SWAT): responsible for responding to critical incidents and
executing warrants.

FY2024 Budget Highlights
The Plantation Police Department is requesting the following positions for the FY2024:
Addition of (1) one Police Officer full-time position. This request has been carefully
reviewed and is needed due to population growth within the City and as part of the long-

Addition of (1) one Senior Code Enforcement Inspector. This request is made due to
population growth and the increased emphasis on addressing code and quality of life
issues in the City.

the Police Department’s independent Communications Unit.
Addition of (1) one Digital Records and License Plate Recognition (LPR) Coordinator to
assist in handling digital records and establishing a Body Worn Camera (BWC) program.

years old.

years.

================================================================================

ARE FALSE POSITIVES and NOT related to Automated License Plate Readers.

document management system:
  - "DP LPR Input" = "Line Printer Remote (LPR) Protocol" input module
  - This is a technical protocol for legacy printer connectivity
  - NOT surveillance technology
  - Part of document processing/workflow software pricing
  - Cost: $200 (software module license), not surveillance equipment

This was a terminology coincidence — "LPR" in this context means "Line Printer
Remote" (legacy printing standard), not "License Plate Reader" (surveillance).

Do NOT include these items in ALPR procurement analysis.
