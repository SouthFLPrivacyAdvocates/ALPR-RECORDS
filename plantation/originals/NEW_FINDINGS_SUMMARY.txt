**DISCOVERY 1: JAG GRANT RESOLUTION FOR PARK LPR CAMERAS**

Document: 2017-11-15 Council Agenda Packet (Page 24)
Type: RESOLUTION (official approval)
Content: Federal grant application for LPR cameras at city parks

Key Details:
- Funding Source: Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant (JAG)
  from U.S. Department of Justice
- Award Amount: $21,743.00
- Implementing Agency: Broward Sheriff's Office (BSO) as lead agent
- Grant Administration Cost: 8.62% to BSO ($1,872.00)
- Net Amount to Plantation: $19,871.00 (approximately $19,868.54 referenced earlier)
- Purpose: "Purchase and installation of License Plate Recognition (LPR)
  cameras at two City parks"
- Approval Deadline: September 30, 2018 for project completion
- No matching funds required
- Status: APPROVED (Resolution passed)

Significance: This confirms the LPR speed trailer park deployment was federally
funded through JAG grant mechanism, not local impact fees.

UNRESOLVED QUESTIONS ANSWERED BY NEW FINDINGS
================================================================================

Q: Where did the $19,868.54 come from?
A: Edward Byrne Memorial JAG grant ($21,743.00 award, minus 8.62% BSO 
   administration = $19,871.00 net). This funded park LPR camera installation,
   not the speed trailers.

Q: What does Vetted Security Solutions do?
A: Acts as system integrator/equipment vendor for the 2 LPR speed trailers
   and provides integration with Vigilant Solutions data platform. Bid winner
   in competitive procurement process (WB No. 019-17).

Q: What is the Vigilant Solutions relationship?
A: Provides central ALPR database and data platform access. Contracted annually
   at $5,300/year (FY2021-2023 documented). Provides "24+ million detections
   per month" data pool for law enforcement queries.

Q: Is there city council oversight?
A: Yes - explicit council resolution approvals for JAG grant (2017-11-15 agenda),
   procurement approvals (2017-06-14 agenda), and ongoing budget line items in
   adopted budgets (FY2021-2024).

Q: Who funds the different ALPR systems?
A: Impact Fees (speed trailers), Federal JAG grant (park cameras), Gateway
   Special District (SR7 corridor), Midtown Special District (Midtown system).

================================================================================
SUMMARY OF FINDINGS
================================================================================

The detailed extraction reveals a multi-faceted, federally-supported ALPR
infrastructure deployment by the City of Plantation:

1. Equipment Procurement (2017): $79,410 from Impact Fees for 2 mobile LPR
   speed trailers through Vetted Security Solutions

2. Park Deployment (2017-2018): $21,743 federal JAG grant for LPR camera
   installation at two city parks

3. Data Platform (Ongoing): $5,300/year subscription to Vigilant Solutions
   for centralized ALPR database access (24M+ monthly detections)

4. Corridor Systems: Gateway and Midtown Districts funding separate ALPR
   systems for those geographic areas (costs undisclosed)

5. Staff Support: FY2024 addition of dedicated LPR Coordinator position
   to manage system enhancement and operations

6. Regional Coordination: Possible participation in City of Pembroke Pines
   LPR technology initiative (connection unclear, needs clarification)

All procurement followed formal processes with council approval, competitive
bidding, and documented funding sources. Ongoing platform relationships are
budgeted and visible in city financial documents.
