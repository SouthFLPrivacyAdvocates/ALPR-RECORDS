MISSION

Agency (CRA) is to pursue revitalization

opportunities, enhance the tax base, encourage

affordable housing, and to facilitate

MESSAGE FROM
THE CHAIR

The vision of the Davie Community Redevelopment Agency (CRA)
is rapidly being fulfilled within the redevelopment area. From the
pedestrian-friendly improvements along our major corridor to the
installation of lush landscape medians and decorative light poles
with themed banners, the progress is evident. These changes
uplift our community and positively contribute to our quaint and
charming hometown appeal here in Cowboy Town, USA.

The redevelopment and improvement of Downtown Davie have
always been at the heart of the CRA. In 2022, we reached
significant milestones and are reaping the successes of the many
years of labor we've invested. The Davie Road Corridor runs
through the center of our downtown, therefore improving the
conditions of this corridor was paramount to creating a lucrative
environment that fosters business development and mixed-use
projects.

Through partnerships with the Town, we have been able to offer our CRA businesses new resources and
programs to help promote economic development. Leveraging substantial incentives, we have brought
public-private partnerships to the table and approved development plans to redevelop key parcels along
Davie Road. Day by day, the progress made cultivates a sense of place that is being enjoyed by all
residents, business owners, and patrons. The projects of the CRA are imprinting a more walkable,
attractive, and engaging feel to our downtown.

This Annual Report captures our proudest accomplishments of 2022 and our future aspirations. We hope
it will also give you a sense of the teamwork, productivity, and persistence that has carried us this far.
Though change takes time, we are confident we are headed in the right direction and encourage you to

come to see the change for yourself!

Judy Paul
CRA Chair

INCENTIVES

The CRA can offer ways to offset development costs and promote redevelopment efforts through
the use of incentives. Below are the incentives currently offered by the Davie CRA:

Commercial Property Improvement

Program Grants (CPIPG)

A reimbursement grant created to promote the rehabilitation and revitalization of
existing commercial, office, and/or multifamily residential rental units.

The Major Grant offers one dollar for every three dollars spent on eligible
hard construction costs for a maximum award of $100,000.

: The Minor Grant offers one dollar for every two dollars spent of eligible
hard construction costs for a maximum of $10,000.

Impact Fees

The CRA can offer to pay the required impact fees generated at the start of a new
construction project. These fees pay for the increase of services anticipated by the
proposed development.

Inclusionary Housing
The CRA can contribute towards the Affordable Housing Trust Fund for new
developments providing over 100 units that opt to pay in lieu of providing
affordable housing units.

Land Donations & Price Reductions

The CRA can offer to donate land or acquire and resell land at a reduced price to
help offset development costs.

>" been constructed, can be leveraged by the CRA as a redevelopment incentive for

developers.

TOWN PARTNERSHIPS

The Town of Davie has partnered with the CRA
to provide exceptional business development
resources through the Small Business
Development Center (SBDC) and public services
such as Community Policing, Code
Enforcement, and Public Works. These
partnerships are vital to ensuring the
redevelopment area maintains its quality and
development readiness.

COMMUNITY POLICING UNIT

The CRA Community Policing Unit is dedicated to
tackling issues that negatively impact the
neighborhoods and businesses within the
redevelopment area. By building relationships with
residents, property owners, business owners and/or
operators, this unit stays apprised of the issues
concerning the community. Their vigilance deters and
addresses trespassing, loitering, drugs, intoxication,
and other related matters.

CODE COMPLIANCE

Through partnerships with the Town, the Town’s Code
Compliance Division focuses on matters that affect the
appearance and quality of the redevelopment area.
Overseeing the physical and social elements of the
downtown area is crucial to assuring redeveloped
properties remain well-maintained and that nuisances
are eliminated. The active presence of the code
inspectors relays to residents, property owners,
business owners and operators that cleanliness and
order are a top priority of the CRA.

PUBLIC WORKS

The Town’s Public Works Department undertakes the
tasks to maintain and assist with improvements
within the redevelopment area. By prioritizing the
redevelopment area’s well-being, the Public Works
Department ensures the public areas within the
downtown stay clear of hazardous conditions and
blighted appearances. Their aid in upkeeping the
appearance and functionality of public property and
improvements is fundamental, as the influx of new
residents and businesses increases their need to
respond to our dynamic redevelopment efforts.

include consultation and advisory pertaining to capital assistance, business planning, marketing and
advertising, procurement, and financial assessments.
